Adders (Homework 3)

Half Adder

A half adder is a way to add two single bits together. A bit can only be equal to 1 or 0, so the max value for adding two bits together is 2. The "S" output of the half adder is called the sum and can be looked at as the "ones" digit of the ouput. The "C" output of the half adder is called the carry and can be looked at as the "twos" digit.

Full Adder

The full adder combines two half adders to allow the addition of three bits together instead of just two. This is important because in order to chain large addition together, the adder must be able to take the two input bits as well as the carried bit from the previous member of the chain.

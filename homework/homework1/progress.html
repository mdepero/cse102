Progress We've Made With Data Storage and Transfer

Initial Values

According to the blu-ray discs wikipedia article, a standard Blu-Ray Disc contains two 25GB layers for a total of 50GB.

According to the Bell 103 Modem wikipedia article (which includes the 300-Baud modem), a typical bit rate would be 300 bit/s.

Conversion

The easiest way to divide 50GB by 300 bits/second is get 50 Gigabytes in terms of just bits. A bit is a single 1 or 0 in a computer. A byte consists of 8 of these bits.

A gigabyte is approximately 1,000,000,000 (or 109) bytes, however this is not exact. Although giga means 109, this is only an approximation of the number of bytes due to the way numbers work in computers.

Instead we much look at it in the sense that a kilobyte (kilo normally meaning 1000) is actually 210 which equals 1024 bytes and then go from there.

8 bits/byte X 1024 bytes/kilobytes X 1000 kilobytes/megabyte X 1000 gigabytes/megabyte x 50 gigabytes = 429,496,729,600 bits This is the total number of bits we are working with for the blu-ray disc.
